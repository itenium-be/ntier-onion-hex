# N-Tier, Hexagonal & Onion Architecture

## Abstract

An introduction to dependency-inverted architectures. Starting from the classic N-Tier/Multitier approach, we explore its strengths and limitations, then move to Ports-and-Adapters, Hexagonal, and Onion Architecture — showing how they all center the domain and manage complexity through dependency inversion.

## Target Audience

Developers and architects looking to understand the spectrum of layered and domain-centered architectures and when to apply each.

## Key Takeaways

- Good architecture makes it easy to do the right thing
- Dependency Inversion is the foundation of modern architectures
- N-Tier is a solid starting point but not a top-tier architecture for large systems
- Ports-and-Adapters / Hexagonal / Onion are all variations on the same theme
- Choose based on team, project, and domain complexity

## Session Format

60 minutes
